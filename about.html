Kathryn Johnstone as...

The Fenland Piper

Kathryn is dynamic, creative and innovative musician with her roots firmly set in the folk music genre, based in the Peterborough and Cambridgeshire area.

With over 10 years experience, and appearances at a variety of prestigious events, you will often find Kathryn playing the Great Highland Bagpipes. Known locally as The Fenland Piper, she has appeared on the BBC news and radio multiple times.

Kathryn loves to share her talent with the community, offering private bagpipe lessons. She has also played regular recitals in her front garden for the residents of Whittlesey during the Covid Lockdowns.

If you would love to have an extra special touch to your event, please do not hesitate to get in contact to discuss your requirements and tune requests.

A multi-instrumentalist

You may be dancing around to the excellent music of "Dennis Cuddles Starlight Band" or "Head In The Sand Ceilidh Band / Duet" at their excellent, upbeat, and energetic ceilidhs and performances where Kathryn plays the melodeon and alto saxophone as well as a variety of other instruments.

A prolific songwriter and composer.

Kathryn is not afraid to push the boundaries of folk music in an enigma of new and exciting songs and tunes. She is hoping to record some of her tunes in the near future, so watch this space.

Quirky and unique

Kathryn also co-owns a little 21 note Trueman street organ, affectionately named "Vanessa", along with Andrew the little toy monkey who accompanies Vanessa on her adventures. With over 50 tune books to run through, Vanessa is an excellent addition to any event. Joyous and cheerful, Vanessa could put a smile on anyone's face.
